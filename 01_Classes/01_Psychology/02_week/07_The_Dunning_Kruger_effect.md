## 🧠 Main Terms & Concepts
* **Core idea** is Dunning-Kruger effect = when people with low skill in an area think they’re doing a good job because they dont know enough to see they arent
	* Its about a gap between:
		* Actual performance (low)
		* Self-judgment (too high)
*   **Dunning-Kruger effect:** When people with low skill in an area tend to overestimate how good they are because they dont yet know enough to see their own mistakes.
*   **Metacognition:** your ability to judge your own thinking and performance.

---

## 🔬 Studies & Experiments
### Study: [The Dunning-Kruger-Study(1999)]
*   **Researcher(s):** Dunning and Kruger
*   **Purpose/Hypothesis:** Gave people tests in:
		* grammer
		* logic
		* humor
	* then asked: “How well do you think you did compared to others?(percentile estimate)”
	* Key results:
		* People in the bottom 25%(lowest scores):
			* Did badly on the tests
			* But rated themselves around average or above
			* → low ability → inflated self-assessment
		* Top performers:
			* Sometimes slightly underestimated themselves(“if it was easy for me, its probably easy for everyone)
	* Why this happens(mechanism)
		* You need some skill to accuratly judge themselves (“if it was easy for me, its probably easy for everone)
	* This is a metacognition issue:
		* Metacognition = ability to judge your own thinking/performance
	* The “double burden”
		* You lack the skill
		* And you lack the ability to see that you lack the skill
	* Mnemonic: “Cant rate till you can do”
		* You cant rate your ability well until you can actually do the skill reasinably well
*   **Methodology:** 
	* Low skill → confidence high (big overstatement)
	* medium skill → confidence drops a bit (“whoa, this is harder than i thought)
	* High skill → Confidence grows again, but not wildly
		* psychologists call this metacognition

## **5. Real-life connections**
**Real‑world examples**

- New driver thinking they’re “one of the safest drivers around”
- Beginner trader with one lucky win thinking, “This is easy, I can beat the market”
- New photographer thinking their phone pics are “almost professional”

Pattern every time:

- **Beginner**
- **Thinks they’re very good**
- **Doesn’t see their mistakes yet**
- **How to protect yourself from it**

- **Get external feedback** (teachers, code reviews, tests, mentors)
- **Practice without help**, then check results
- Compare yourself to **clear standards**, not just your feelings
- Remember: feeling “I have so much to learn” often means your **metacognition is improving**, not that you’re failing

## 📝 Summary (Active Recall)
**Dunning–Kruger effect (in plain language)**

- When someone **isn’t very good** at something but **thinks they’re doing a good job**
- They **don’t know enough to see their own mistakes**, so their **confidence is higher than their actual skill**

**Kruger & Dunning (1999) study**

- People took tests in **grammar, logic, and humor**
- Then guessed how well they did compared to others
- The **worst performers**:
    - **Scored low** on the tests
    - But **rated themselves as average or better**
    - → **Low ability → inflated self‑assessment**

**Why it happens (metacognition)**

- You need **some skill** to judge your **own skill**
- Beginners have a **“double burden”**:
    - They lack the skill
    - And they lack the ability to **notice** that they lack the skill

Mnemonic: **“Can’t rate till you can do.”**
