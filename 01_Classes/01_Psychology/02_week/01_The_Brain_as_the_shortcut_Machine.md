## 🧠 Main Terms & Concepts
* **Heuristic:** A mental shortcut our brain uses to make quick decisions without doing full analysis →
	* Our brain trades speed for accuracy
	* The COST: Sometimes we loose accuracy and  make biased decisions
	* Mental Shortcut
	* A tool for decision making
	* They’re fast and often useful, but frequently lead to mistakes
* **Bias:** A predictable mistake in judgment that comes from relying on a shortcut. →
	* Systemic error
	* Arises from uses of Heuristics
* Heuristic → Shortcut :: bias → The patterned error it can cause
*  **Representativeness Heuristics:** Judging how likely something is by how much it looks like a typical case or stereotype.
*  **Availability Heuristics:** Judging how something is by easily examples come to mind.
*  **Confirmation bias/ Confirmation Heuristic:** We look for, and remember information that supports what we already believe, and we ignore or downplay anything that goes against it.

## 🔬 Studies & Experiments
### Study: [The predictable irrational mind]
*   **Researcher(s):** **Daniel Kahneman** and **Amos Tversky**
*   **Purpose/Hypothesis:** Their classic 1974 paper is where **representativeness**, **availability**, and **confirmation** (as a heuristic/bias) were first laid out together.
*   **Methodology:** The predictable irrational mind
*   **Results:** 
	* **Main result of the research**

Kahneman & Tversky showed that **people don’t always think in a purely logical, statistical way**.  
Instead, we often rely on **heuristics (mental shortcuts)** like:

- **Representativeness** – “Does this look like the stereotype?”
- **Availability** – “Can I easily think of examples?”
- **Confirmation** – “Does this match what I already believe?”

These shortcuts are **fast and usually helpful**, but they can lead to **predictable biases** (systematic errors in judgment).
*   **Conclusions:** 
	* The brain is a **shortcut machine**: it trades **accuracy** for **speed**.
	*  Because we use these shortcuts in similar ways, our mistakes are **not random** — they’re **predictable**.
	* Understanding these heuristics (RAC: Representativeness, Availability, Confirmation) helps us:
	    - Notice when we might be thinking in a biased way
	    - Slow down in important decisions (money, health, relationships)
	    - Make more accurate, fair, and rational choices

	- “Close enough, this _looks like_ the right pattern” → **representativeness**
	- “This is easy to remember, so it must be common” → **availability**
	- “This fits what I already think, so I’ll trust it” → **confirmation**
- Helpful mnemonic (if any):
	-  *R*-Representativeness Heuristics → “Looks like a stereotype”
	-  *A*-Availability Heuristics → “Comes to mind Easily”
	-  *C*-Confirmation bias/heuristics → “Agrees with what i already believe”
- RAC = **R**epresentativeness, **A**vailability, **C**onfirmation
