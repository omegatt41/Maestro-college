## 🧠 Main Terms & Concepts
*   **Inattentional Blindness:** Failure to see something obviuos because your attention is locked on something else.
	* Failure to see something cause your attention is on something else
*   **Attention:** Attention is limited, So focusing on a singular task can limit us, causing us to miss something obvious
*  **Selective Attention:** - You choose to focus on one thing like a text or counting ball passes
	* You can only focus deeply on limited things
	* Our brain focuses on one thing and filters out the rest
	* Example → Doing schoolwork while watching a movie
*  **The task Focus:** Attention on a specific task
* IV **Independent Variable:** What the experimenter Controls/manipulates(a task focused on)
* DV **Dependent Variable:** What a researcher measures to see if IF had and effect
*  **Delayed Gratification** Choosing  a later, bigger benefit over and immediate smaller one
*  **Self Control:** stopping or changing an impulse so you can stick to a goal i care about.
*  **Impulse:** A do it now feeling
*  **Goal:** “What i really want”
* Self Control is a skill Willpower is the fuel for that skill
*  **Cognitive Reframing** Pretending or imagining the thing differently in our mind so it feels less tempting.
*  **Situational Modification** Changing the physical setup so its harder to give in.(changing your environment so something is less tempting)

## 🔬 Studies & Experiments
### Study: [**Simons & Chabris “Invisible Gorilla” study (1999)**]
*   **Researcher(s):** Siamon and Chabris(1999)
*   **Purpose/Hypothesis:** To show how our brain has limited resources to focus on a number things at once
*   **Methodology:**
	* - Participants watch people passing basketballs.
- Task: **Count passes** made by players in white shirts.
- During the video, a person in a **gorilla suit** walks through, stops, thumps chest, exits.
- Many people do **not** notice the gorilla on the first watch.
- This shows **inattentional blindness**:
    - Focus on counting passes → miss a huge, unexpected event.

## **5. Real-life connections**
- Inattentional blindness matters for **safety**:
    - Driving + phone → miss other cars, cyclists, or pedestrians.
    - Work + distraction → miss errors, warnings, or important details.
- Key idea: if you’re doing **two attention-heavy tasks**, something will probably get **missed**.
