## 🔬 Studies & Experiments
### Study: [The Iowa Gambling Task]
*   **Researcher(s):** 
*   **Purpose/Hypothesis:** IN this task, people pick cards from four different decks to try to win money. Some decks are secretly “bad” (big wins but even bigger losses), and some are “good” (smaller wins, smaller losses, but safer overall).
*   **Methodology:** 
	* 4 Decks of cards some good some bad 
	* When you consider drawing from a bad deck, your body starts to show a small stress reaction(like a mini “uh-oh”)
	* That signal nudges you away from the bad deck, even if you cant yet explain why.
	* In real life, this feels like:
		* Tight chest, slight nausea, or tension = “maybe dont do this"
		* Calm, open, steady feeling = “this seems okay”
	* Somantic Marker Hypothesis
		* Somantic marker - body signal(like a feeling in your stomach, tight chest, sweating) that tags an option as good or bad
		* These signals can guide choices before conscious reasoning kicks in
*   **Results:** 
	* When you get slightly stressed or anxious, your sweat glands activate a bit, and your skin conducts electricity more. That tiny change can be measured.
*   **Conclusions:** 
* What they found:
	* Before people knew which decks were bad
	* Their skin conductance went up when they hovered over or drew from the bad decks.
	* As if the body was quietly saying, “Careful with this one,” before the person could explain it.
Those early body warnings are called anticipatory somantic markers - your body “marks” a future option as risky

- **Skin conductance / anticipatory signal**
    
    - In the IGT, researchers measured **skin conductance** (tiny changes in sweating).
    - This went **up** when people were about to pick from bad decks, _even before_ they knew which decks were risky.
    - That early rise is an **anticipatory somatic marker** = the body’s warning light.
- **Emotion + rational decisions**
    
    - Emotions aren’t always irrational; they can **support rational choices**.
    - Fast body-based signals help you steer away from risky options that logic hasn’t fully explained yet.

### Study: [Somatic Marker Hypothesis]
*   **Researcher(s):** Antonio Damasio
*   **Purpose/Hypothesis:** Our body tags options as “good” or “bad” with physical signals (somatic = body) - like a tiny jolt of stress or a relaxed feeling - before you can put it into words
* 1. **Basic idea**
    
    > Your **body** sends signals (somatic markers) that “tag” choices as _good_ or _bad_ **before** you’re fully aware of why.
    
2. **What is a somatic marker?**
    
    - _Somatic_ = **body**
    - _Marker_ = **tag or signal**
    - It’s a **physical feeling** tied to a past experience (good or bad) that shows up when you face a similar choice again.
    - Examples: tight stomach, sweaty hands, relaxed chest, light feeling in body.
3. **How it works in decisions**
    
    - When you think about a risky option, your brain activates the old memory of something similar.
    - That memory triggers a **body reaction** (somatic marker).
    - The body reaction then nudges you:
        - bad tag → “avoid this”
        - good tag → “go for it”
    - This happens **fast**, before slow, logical thinking finishes.
4. **In the Iowa Gambling Task**
    
    - Bad decks are linked to losses → body learns “these are dangerous.”
    - Before the person can say “this deck is bad,” their **skin conductance** (sweating) increases when they go near those decks.
    - That rise is an **anticipatory somatic marker** = early warning signal.
5. **Key point for exams**
    
    - Somatic Marker Hypothesis = **emotions and body states help guide rational decisions**, especially under uncertainty, by marking options as good/bad before conscious reasoning.
## **4. Why people do this (explanations)**

Mnemonic:
Use the mnemonic **B-TAG**: your **Body TAGs** options as good or bad.

**B – Body**

- Somatic = body.
- Somatic markers are **body signals** (stomach, sweating, tension, etc.).

**T – Tags**

- The body **tags** options as good/bad based on past experience.

**A – Anticipates**

- The signal comes **before** you can explain it (anticipatory warning).

**G – Guides decisions**

- Those body tags **guide** your choices toward safer / better options.

So:

> **Somatic Marker Hypothesis = B‑TAG: Body Tags, Anticipates, Guides.**
