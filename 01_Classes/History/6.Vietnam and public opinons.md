### 3. Major Events & Timeline
*Note chronological developments.*
- Early stage: U.S. sent money, weapons, and advisers to South Vietnam.
- Mid-1960s: U.S. troop levels increased sharply.
- 1968: **Tet Offensive** shocked Americans and damaged confidence.
- Later: U.S. withdrew; South Vietnam eventually fell to North Vietnam.

### 4. Cause and Effect
*What sparked these events, and what were the consequences?*
- Vietnam was heavily covered on television.
- Americans could visually see battlefield destruction and civilian suffering.
- This often clashed with official claims that the war was going well.
- That mismatch created a **credibility gap**: distrust between citizens and government.
#### Outcome and long term impact:
- The U.S. withdrew from Vietnam.
- South Vietnam fell to North Vietnam.
- The war damaged trust in government and made Americans more skeptical of military intervention.
#### Cause and Effect chain:
	Cold War fear → 
	U.S. escalation → 
	media exposes reality → 
	credibility gap grows → 
	protests increase → U.S. withdraws → 
	South Vietnam falls → 
	long-term distrust in government

## Key Terms & Concepts
- **Containment:** U.S. policy of trying to stop the spread of communism.
- **Escalation:** Increasing military involvement.
- **Tet Offensive:** 1968 North Vietnamese/Viet Cong attacks that shocked the U.S. public.
- **Credibility gap:** The gap between what officials said and what the public believed.
- **Antiwar movement:** Public protest movement against continuing the war.
