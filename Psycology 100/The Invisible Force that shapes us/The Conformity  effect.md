# 2026-03-30 - The conformity effect


## 📌 Overview
*   **Key Themes:** _Why do people change their answers or behavior just to match a group, even when the group is clearly wrong?_
*   **Relevant Biological Processes:** Asch’s line-judgment conformity experiment
*   **Key Theorists/Researchers:** 
	* Solomon Asch (1951)

---

## 🧠 Main Concepts & Definitions
*   **Conformity :** Changing behavior, answer or opinion  to match the group.
	* Usually because of real or imagined social pressure
	* In Asch's experiment many of the participants knew the group was wrong but said a wrong answer to conform and not feel like an  outsider
		* there are 2 reasons to conform 1, be liked or accepted 2, wanting to be correct

*   **Group pressure:** The feeling you should go along with what the group is doing or saying to conform to not stand out

*  **Normative Social Influence:** "The need to belong", You go along with the group so people will like you, accept you, or so you don’t look weird or stand out! 
	* Ex: Laughing at a joke you don’t think is funny or dont understand  because everyone else thinks its funny
* **Informational Influence:** Information need. You go along with the group because you think they might be **right** and you might be **wrong**.
	* Ex: Unsure about an answer on a hard test, so you choose what most others seem to pick because you think they know something you dont.

## 🔬 Studies & Experiments
### Study: [Asch’s line-judgment conformity experiment]
*   **Researcher(s):**  Soloman Asch
*   **Purpose/Hypothesis:** Why do people change their answers or behaviors to conform to the group, even if the group is wrong!
* **Goal:** To see if people would conform to a group’s wrong answer **even when the correct answer was obvious**.
* **Methodology of Asch’s Line-Judgment Conformity Experiment (1951)**
- **Participants:**  
    Small groups of about 7–9 people.  
    In each group, **only 1 was a real participant**; the rest were **confederates** (actors working with the experimenter).
- **Task:**  
    Everyone saw:
    - 1 **standard line**
    - 3 **comparison lines**  
        They had to say out loud which comparison line matched the standard line in length.  
        The task was **very easy** when done alone.
- **Procedure:**
    - People answered **in order**, out loud.
    - The real participant usually answered **near the end** of the row.
    - On some trials, the confederates were told to all give the **same wrong answer** on purpose.
    - The key question: would the real participant **stick with the obvious correct answer** or **conform to the wrong group answer**?
- **Key variables in the method:**
    - **Group unanimity:** all confederates agreed on the same wrong answer.
    - **Number of confederates:** several people against the one real participant.
    - **Public responding:** answers were **spoken out loud**, not written privately.
*   **Results(what they found to be true):**
	* Many participants **conformed at least once** and gave the wrong answer just to match the group.
	* **Unanimity mattered:** when the group was **fully unanimous** in the wrong answer, conformity was high.
	* **Presence of an ally:** if even **one other person** disagreed with the group, conformity dropped sharply. 
*   **Conclusions(What it means):** 
	* Use the mnemonic(aiding or designed to aid the memory.):
	* Why people conform:
		* **N**: Need to Belong : => Normative Social influence
			* Main motive: _“I don’t want to stand out or be rejected.”_
			* Driven by: desire for **approval**, fear of **judgment** or exclusion.
			* Often know the group is wrong but go along anyway.
		* **I**: Informational Need : => Informational Social Influence
			*  Main motive: _“They probably know more than I do.”_
			*  Driven by: **uncertainty**, thinking others have better info.
			* You genuinely think the group might be **correct**.
	* Conformity VS Obedience:
		* **Conformity:** => peer pressure
			* Influence from people at your **own level** (friends, classmates, coworkers).
			* No formal authority is needed.
			* Example: Dressing a certain way because everyone at school does.
		* **Obedience = authority pressure:**   
			*  Influence from someone **above you** (boss, teacher, officer).
			*   Based on **power / status / orders**.*
			* Example: Doing extra work because your boss directly tells you to.

## 📝 Summary (Active Recall)
**High-yield bullets for quick review**

- Conformity = changing to match a group.
- Asch (1951): people gave obviously wrong answers to match a unanimous group.
- **Unanimity** increases conformity; **one ally** reduces it.
- **Normative influence (N = Need to belong):** go along to be liked / avoid rejection.
- **Informational influence (I = Information needed):** go along because you think others are right.
- Conformity = peer pressure; Obedience = authority pressure.
