## 🧠 Main Terms & Concepts
*   **Clear Right to widthdraw:** Participants can stop their participation at any time without any reason, without penalty or pressure

	* What they’ll be asked to do
	* Risks or discomforts

> **C.I.D.E.R.** = main tools to protect people in research

- **C – Consent (Informed consent)**
    
    - Tell participants, _before_ the study:
        - general **purpose** (e.g., “study of obedience / decision-making”)
        - basic **procedures** (what they’ll do, how long)
        - possible **risks/discomforts** (e.g., stress, frustration)
        - that it’s **voluntary**
    - They **agree** (usually by signing a form).
    - Milgram problem: people weren’t told they might believe they were seriously harming someone.
- **I – IRB (Institutional Review Board)**
    
    - A **committee** that reviews studies _before_ they happen.
    - Checks that:
        - risks are as low as possible
        - benefits are worth the risks (**risk–benefit / ethics cost**)
        - consent, withdrawal, debriefing, and confidentiality are handled correctly.
- **D – Debrief**
    
    - After the study, especially if there was **deception** or stress:
        - explain the **true purpose**
        - reveal any **deception**
        - reassure (e.g., “no one was actually harmed,” “many people reacted like you”)
        - answer questions and offer support if needed.
- **E – Ethics cost (Risk–benefit & minimal risk)**
    
    - Don’t expose people to more risk than **everyday life**, unless:
        - there is a strong scientific **reason**, and
        - there are strong **protections**.
    - Emotional/psychological harm _counts_ as real risk.
- **R – Right to withdraw**
    
    - Participants can **stop at any time**, for **any reason**,
        - with **no penalty**
        - no pressure to keep going
        - still get **paid** if payment was promised.
    - Milgram problem: experimenter said things like,
        - “The experiment requires that you continue” → violates this right.

## Flash Cards →
	**Flashcard 1**  
**Q:** What is **informed consent**?  
**A:** Telling participants the general purpose, what they’ll do, possible risks, that it’s voluntary, and then getting their agreement (usually in writing).

**Flashcard 2**  
**Q:** What is the **right to withdraw**?  
**A:** Participants can stop the study at any time, for any reason, with no penalty or pressure, and still get what they were promised (e.g., payment).

**Flashcard 3**  
**Q:** What does **IRB** stand for, and what does it do?  
**A:** **Institutional Review Board** – a committee that reviews studies before they run to check risks, benefits, consent, withdrawal, debriefing, and confidentiality.

**Flashcard 4**  
**Q:** What is **debriefing**?  
**A:** Explaining the true purpose of the study at the end, revealing any deception, reassuring participants (e.g., no one was harmed), and answering questions.

**#flashcards**  
**Q:** What is **minimal risk / risk–benefit (ethics cost)**?::  
**A:** Don’t expose people to more harm than everyday life unless there’s a strong reason and strong protections; emotional stress counts as real risk.

- **C** – Consent (informed consent)
