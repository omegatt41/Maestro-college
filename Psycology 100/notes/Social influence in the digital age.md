# 2026-04-03 - 📌  [[Social influence in the digital age]]
*   **Key Themes:** Modern media constantly uses a small set of predictable psychological levers (Cialdini’s six principles) to nudge our choices — and once you can _see_ them, you can resist manipulation and choose more freely.

*   **Key Theorists/Researchers:** 
	* **[[Robert Cialdini]]**, a social psychologist.

---

## 🧠 Main Terms & Concepts
*   **[[Reciprocity]]:** When someone gives us something(a favor, gift, freebie), we feel a pull to give something back - a click a follow, an email address, a purchase.

*   **[[Social proof]]:** when were unsure what to do , we look at what many other people seem to be doing and that follow that. 

*  **[[Scarcity]]:** we value things more when they seem rare,, limited , or about too disappear.

*  **[[Liking]]:** were more likely to say yes to people we like, feel close to or find attractive/relatable.

*  **[[Authority]]:** were more likely to follow requests or believe information when it comes from someone who seems like an expert or has high status.

* **Commitment and consistency** = once we take a small stand or action we feel pressure to consistent with it later.
- Key distinctions (e.g., A vs. B):
- Social Proof vs Normative Conformity
    - A = Social Proof → “If many people do it, Its probably right, or Best”
    
    - B = Normative Conformity → “ Ill go along so i don’t feel left out, or Weird”

## **5. Real-life connections**
**When Persuasion Becomes Manipulation (Ethical Red Flags)**

Persuasion is normal; it becomes **unethical** when:

- The information is **false** or **misleading**
    - Fake timers (“Offer ends in 1 hour!” but restarts every time)
    - Fake scarcity (“Only 10 spots left” for an unlimited online class)
- The pressure is **too intense** or hides important details
    - “Buy now” before you can see full terms, prices, or cancel rules
- It targets people’s **vulnerabilities** without giving them a fair chance to decide

Your stock‑trading ad example:

- “Only 10 slots left, grab it now” on an unlimited online class  
    → Uses **Scarcity**  
    → Likely **manipulative**, because the limit is unnecessary or fake.

## 📝 Summary (Active Recall)
**[[Cialdini’s 6 Principles of Persuasion]] (Digital Age) – Brief Summary**

1. **Reciprocity**  
    When someone gives us something, we feel we should give back.
    
    - Online: free guides, free trials, “bonus” PDFs → drive sign‑ups and clicks.
2. **Commitment & Consistency**  
    After a small “yes,” we feel pressure to stay consistent with it.
    
    - Online: “just enter your email,” tiny goals, free opt‑ins → later upsells.
3. **Social Proof**  
    We look at what many others do and follow the majority.
    
    - Online: “50,000 users,” “best seller,” “most popular,” like/view counts.
4. **Liking**  
    We say yes more easily to people we like or relate to.
    
    - Online: influencer promos, friendly creators saying “I use this myself.”
5. **Authority**  
    We trust and follow people who seem like experts.
    
    - Online: “doctor‑recommended,” certifications, badges, official logos.
6. **Scarcity**  
    We value things more when they seem limited or about to end.
    
    - Online: “only 3 left,” countdown timers, “offer ends tonight,” “limited edition.”

**Ethics:**  
Persuasion becomes **manipulation** when info is fake or misleading (e.g., fake “10 slots left” for an unlimited online class), or when it uses strong pressure while hiding important details.
